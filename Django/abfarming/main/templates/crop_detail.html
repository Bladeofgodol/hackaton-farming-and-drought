Teff

some information about Teff


Teff is a grain crop that is native to Ethiopia and is widely cultivated and consumed in the country. It is a small, gluten-free grain that is packed with nutrients, including protein, dietary fiber, iron, and calcium. Teff is also a good source of resistant starch, which has been linked to several health benefits, including improved digestion and reduced risk of chronic diseases. Teff is traditionally used to make injera, a fermented flatbread that is a staple in Ethiopian cuisine. It is also used to make porridges, stews, and other traditional dishes. Teff has gained popularity in recent years as a health food and gluten-free alternative to wheat flour.
